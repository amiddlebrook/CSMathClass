Section 0

Why Derivatives Matter

In CS, we optimize. We want to find the minimum error, the maximum efficiency, or the optimal path. The derivative tells us the direction of steepest ascent (or descent).

üé¢ Simple: Imagine a roller coaster. The derivative at any moment is exactly how steep the track is right now.
  • Positive slope = Going up
  • Zero slope = Top of the hill (Maximum) or Bottom (Minimum)
  • Negative slope = Going down
Section 1

The Definition

The derivative is just the slope formula ($\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$) where the two points get infinitely close together.

The Limit Definition
f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}
Section 2

The Power Rule

You almost never use the limit definition. You use shortcuts.

Power Rule
\frac{d}{dx} x^n = n x^{n-1}
\text{Examples:} \newline \frac{d}{dx} x^2 = 2x \newline \frac{d}{dx} x^3 = 3x^2 \newline \frac{d}{dx} \sqrt{x} = \frac{d}{dx} x^{0.5} = 0.5 x^{-0.5} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}
Section 3

The Chain Rule

This is the most important rule for Machine Learning (Backpropagation).

Chain Rule
\frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)
‚õìÔ∏è Simple: "Derivative of the Outside" times "Derivative of the Inside".
Example: $(3x+1)^2$
Outside: $u^2 \to 2u$
Inside: $3x+1 \to 3$
Result: $2(3x+1) \cdot 3 = 6(3x+1)$
Section 4

Product & Quotient Rules

Product Rule

\frac{d}{dx} (uv) = u'v + uv'

Mnemonic: "Left d-Right plus Right d-Left"

üìê Simple: Imagine a rectangle with sides u and v.
When both grow, the area change = (new width strip) + (new height strip).
That's u'v (width grows) + uv' (height grows). The tiny corner square is negligible!

Quotient Rule

\frac{d}{dx} (\frac{u}{v}) = \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}

Mnemonic: "Lo d-Hi minus Hi d-Lo, over Lo Lo"
