Section 1

The T-Shaped Model

The curriculum so far has built the horizontal bar of the T: a broad foundation in Math and CS. Now you must build the vertical bar.

Why? Real insights come from depth. You cannot contribute to the boundary of knowledge without going deep.
Section 2

Selection Strategy

How do you choose? Use the Hamming Criterion (Richard Hamming):

  • "What are the most important problems in your field?"
  • "Are you working on one of them?"
  • "If not, why not?"

Also consider Personal Fit: What problems do you think about in the shower? What feels like play to you but work to others?

Section 3

Reading Papers

Stop reading textbooks. Start reading papers.

  1. Pass 1 (Title/Abstract/Intro): Is this relevant?
  2. Pass 2 (Figures/Theorems): What is the main result?
  3. Pass 3 (Details): Re-derive the proofs.
Section 4

Active Problem Solving

Don't just read. Solve.

The Feynman Algorithm: 1. Write down the problem. 2. Think very hard. 3. Write down the solution.

Jokes aside, keep a research notebook. Write down questions, not just answers.

Section 5

Community

Science is social. Attend seminars (even if you don't understand). Talk to people. The "invisible college" is where the real knowledge transfer happens.
