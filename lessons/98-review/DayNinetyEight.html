Review Mindset

The final days are for refinement. You have the knowledge; now you must ensure it can be retrieved instantly under pressure.

A dull sword can still cut, but a sharp one cuts with zero resistance. You want the math to feel like it's "at your fingertips."

Section 3

Why This Matters in CS

Cache Warming: In high-performance systems, we "warm the cache" by loading the most important data into RAM before the first request hits. Reviewing flashcards is warming your mental cache. When the exam (the request) hits, you don't want to be fetching from the "disk" of long-term memory; you want the data in L1 cache (active recall).

Regression Testing: Every time you learn something new, you risk forgetting something old. Spaced repetition is your regression suite for your brain. It ensures that the new "features" you've added (Day 97) haven't broken the "legacy code" (Day 1).
