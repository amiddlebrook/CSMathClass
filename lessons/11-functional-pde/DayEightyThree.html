Section 1

Hölder's Inequality

The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality generalized for `p` and `q` (`1/p + 1/q = 1`).

||fg||₁ ≤ ||f||_p ||g||_q

If `f ∈ L^p` and `g ∈ L^q`, their product is integrable (`L^1`).

Section 2

Convolution

Convolution is "blurring" one function with another.

(f ∗ g)(x) = ∫ f(y)g(x-y) dy
Young's Inequality: ||f ∗ g||_r ≤ ||f||_p ||g||_q where 1/p + 1/q = 1 + 1/r.

In Fourier land: `(f ∗ g)̂ = f̂ · ĝ`.

Section 3

Smoothing & Approx to Identity

Idea: If `g` is a very narrow, tall "bump" function (integral 1), then `f ∗ g` is very close to `f`, but distinctively smoother.

This allows us to prove density theorems: "Smooth functions are dense in Lᵖ". This is crucial for PDE proofs (prove for smooth, take limits).
