Section 1

Affine Varieties

The solution set of a system of polynomial equations.

V(S) = \{x \in k^n : f(x)=0 \, \forall f \in S \}

Varieties bridge Algebra (Ideal `I`) and Geometry (Set `V`).

Section 2

Grothendieck Revolution

Classical varieties study solutions in a fixed field (like `C`).

Schemes allow solutions in ANY ring (including integers, nilpotent elements). "A scheme assumes the points are not enough."

Section 3

Sheaf Cohomology

Measures the obstruction to gluing local solutions into global solutions.

If `H^1(X, F) = 0`, then local data can always be stitched together globally.

Section 4

Bézout's Theorem

Two projective plane curves of degree `m` and `n` intersect in exactly `mn` points (counting multiplicity).

This is a topological invariant of intersection.
