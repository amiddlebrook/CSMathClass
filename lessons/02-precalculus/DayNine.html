Section 0

Why Trigonometry Matters

It's not just about triangles. In Computer Science, Trigonometry is the math of rotation and waves.

Key Applications:
  • Graphics: Calculating coordinates for 3D rotations, lighting, and cameras.
  • Game Dev: Determining the angle to a target (atan2) or moving an object in a direction.
  • Signal Processing: All audio and image compression (JPEG, MP3) relies on decomposing signals into Sine waves (Fourier Transform).
Section 1

Radians vs Degrees

Degrees (360¬∞) are arbitrary. Radians are natural.

üçï Simple: Degrees are like cutting a pizza into 360 tiny slices because "that sounds nice." Radians are measuring the crust. 1 Radian is when the length of the crust (arc) equals the length of your slice (radius).
360^\circ = 2\pi \text{ radians} \approx 6.28 \text{ radians}
Coder's Note: All standard math libraries (`Math.sin()`, `numpy.sin()`) take input in Radians, not degrees. Keep this in mind or your character will spin wildly!
Section 2

The Unit Circle

The Unit Circle is a circle with radius 1 centered at (0,0). It is the "Master Map" for all trigonometry.

The Coordinates: Any point $(x, y)$ on the circle corresponds to an angle $\theta$:
  • $x = \cos(\theta)$
  • $y = \sin(\theta)$
üé° Simple: Imagine a Ferris Wheel with radius 1.
  • Cosine is how far *left or right* you are from the center.
  • Sine is how *high or low* you are from the center.
\text{At } 0^\circ (0 \text{ rad}): (1, 0) \implies \cos(0)=1, \sin(0)=0 \newline \text{At } 90^\circ (\pi/2 \text{ rad}): (0, 1) \implies \cos(\pi/2)=0, \sin(\pi/2)=1
Section 3

Sin, Cos, Tan

These functions map an angle (input) to a ratio (output).

SOH CAH TOA

  • Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse (On Unit Circle: $y$)
  • Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse (On Unit Circle: $x$)
  • Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent (Slope: $y/x$)
PythonCalculating Position
import math

# Want to move 5 units at a 45 degree angle
radius = 5
angle_deg = 45
angle_rad = math.radians(angle_deg) # Convert first!

x_pos = radius * math.cos(angle_rad)
y_pos = radius * math.sin(angle_rad)

# This gives us the exact vector to move!
Section 4

Pythagorean Identity

Since the Unit Circle coordinates form a right triangle with the radius (hypotenuse) of 1, the Pythagorean Theorem ($a^2 + b^2 = c^2$) gives us the most important identity in Trig:

\sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta) = 1
What this means: No matter what angle you choose, the square of the x-coordinate plus the square of the y-coordinate always sums to 1. This "normalizes" vectors in graphics.
