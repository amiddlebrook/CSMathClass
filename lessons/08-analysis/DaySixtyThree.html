Key Concepts Review

Foundations & Topology

ε-δ Formulations: The rigor behind limits and continuity. Completeness of ℝ: Every Cauchy sequence converges. This distinguishes ℝ from ℚ. Compactness: Heine-Borel theorem (closed + bounded = compact in ℝⁿ). Essential for extremes.

Core Theorems

Three Pillars of Calculus on [a,b]:
  • IVT: Continuous functions skip no values.
  • EVT: Continuous functions on compact sets attain Max/Min.
  • MVT: Differentiable functions have instant slope = avg slope.

Convergence & Spaces

Modes of Convergence: Pointwise ⇐ Uniform (preserves continuity) Standard Lᵖ Convergence (norm based)
Important Spaces: • C[a,b]: Continuous functions (Banach with Sup norm) • L²: Square integrable (Hilbert Space) • Metric Spaces: Generalizing "distance"
