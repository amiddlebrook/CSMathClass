Section 1

Finite Products

If we have spaces `X` and `Y`, the Cartesian product `X \times Y` is the set of pairs `(x, y)`. How do we define the topology?

The "obvious" way is to say a basis for `\tau_{X \times Y}` is sets of the form `U \times V`, where `U \in \tau_X` and `V \in \tau_Y`.

This works perfectly for `\mathbb{R}^2 = \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}`. Open rectangles form a basis.

Section 2

The Product Topology

Technically, the product topology is the coarsest topology that makes the projection maps `\pi_X: X \times Y \to X` and `\pi_Y: X \times Y \to Y` continuous.

A sub-basis is given by `\pi_X^{-1}(U) = U \times Y` and `\pi_Y^{-1}(V) = X \times V`.

Section 3

Infinite Products

What if we have `\prod_{i=1}^\infty X_i`?

We have two choices:

  1. Box Topology: Basis elements are `\prod U_i` where `U_i` is open.
  2. Product Topology: Basis elements are `\prod U_i` where `U_i = X_i` for all but finitely many `i`.
The Box topology contains "too many" open sets. We almost always use the Product Topology because it preserves convergence correctly.
ðŸŽ¯ Simple: Imagine a point in infinite-dimensional space (like a function on [0,1]).
â€¢ Box: "ALL coordinates must be close" â€” too restrictive, almost nothing converges.
â€¢ Product: "Only finitely many coordinates need to be close" â€” matches our intuition for sequences of functions.
Section 4

Tychonoff's Theorem

One of the most profound results in topology.

Theorem: The product of any collection of compact spaces is compact with respect to the product topology.

This holds even for uncountable products! It is equivalent to the Axiom of Choice.

This fails for the Box Topology.

from itertools import product

prod = list(product(X, Y))
print(f"X x Y ({len(prod)} elements):")
print(prod)

# 'Infinite' Product Approximation
# Representing a point in {0, 1}^N as a generator
def infinite_point_generator():
    i = 0
    while True:
        yield 0 if i % 2 == 0 else 1
        i += 1

gen = infinite_point_generator()
print("\nFirst 10 dims of an infinite point:")
print([next(gen) for _ in range(10)])

# Note: In Product Topology, a sequence x_n -> x converges 
# if and only if it converges in each coordinate.
# This is "pointwise convergence".
